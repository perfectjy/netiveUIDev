Corporate Mark

H

  • The corporate mark is based on the English corporate initial H as a motif to express trust and soundness with a simple overall shape.
  • The combination of the horizontal bar in the English letter H and the vertical bar on the right internally symbolizes the corporate will and pride of "First & Best", and externally signifies our top-notch service we provide to our customers as the No.1.
  • The orange color, which is used as the main color, denotes the warm heart and high-quality service of the people of Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance who deal with our customers.
    • It is available for use as an individual mark separated from the logotype and used for general communication productions such as printed materials, web/mobile platforms, and video images.
    • When applying the corporate mark to an app, it must not be modified under any circumstances without prior approval.
    • Minimum dimensions: 5mm

Logotype

현대해상,Hyundai Insurance

  • The logotype is the most basic element that visually communicates the identity of Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance together with the corporate mark, and it serves as a character designed to emphasize the official name.
  • In principle, the logotype should be used in parallel with the corporate mark, but it may also be used on its own
    • It must not be modified without prior approval, but it may be enlarged and reduced in direct proportion.

The signature is a combination of the corporate mark and logotype. In this case, the corporate mark serves as the basic foundation, while the logotype is used to clarify the company name. The signature must not be modified without prior approval, although it may be enlarged or reduced in direct proportion.

Exclusive Color

-Hi Orange:Pantone 1375C,M55 Y100 R241 G141 B0 (#f18d00) -Hi Dark Blue:Pantone 281C,C100 M70 K45 R0 G47 B112 #003070

Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance aims to build a unified corporate image through the consistent use of colors.
Since the base color extracted from the CI colors (Hi Orange and Hi Dark Blue) may vary in terms of brightness, chrominance, and more, depending on the medium, when reproducing the color, the closest color should be used by referring to the specified color value.

  • If a process color other than a specified spot color is required, the specified CMYK color values should be used, while RGB color values should be used for digital and video media.
