Compliance/Internal Control Standards

Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance has appointed a compliance officer to ensure that its employees comply with all laws and regulations while performing their duties, and launched a compliance system to exercise and inspect the internal control at all times. The company has also internal control standards in place as the board of directors passed a vote on October 21, 2000 according to the Insurance Business Act.

Main Duties of the Compliance Officer

  • The compliance officer establishes and manages the internal control standards, and conducts inspections and investigations accordingly.
  • The compliance officer provides legal compliance training for our employees.
  • To ensure that the company's daily work does not deviate from laws and company regulations, the compliance officer conduct a review before any work begins.
  • The compliance officer manages legal affairs such as agreements, conventions, and regulations according to the Insurance Business Act.

How to Operate the Fair Trade Compliance System

  • To effectively ensure fair trade compliance, the board of directors has appointed a compliance officer to assure compliance.
  • The company has established the Regulations on Voluntary Compliance of Fair Trade to regulate and operate the overall compliance.
  • In areas with a high risk of violation, such as sales, we provide training for at least 2 hours every half-year to our employees.
  • We have sanctions in place depending on the degree of violation for employee who violate the Fair Trade Act.
