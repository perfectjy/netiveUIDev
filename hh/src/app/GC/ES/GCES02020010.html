Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance takes part in a variety of initiatives and certifications to rapidly identify ESG issues and actively implement them.
CDP signatories

Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance participated as a signatory to the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP) in 2021. CDP has the longest history among global initiatives associated with climate change, and it is a carbon information disclosure initiative launched in 2000 when financial institutions around the world played the central role by urging companies to disclose management information related to their response to climate change. A party that joins the CDP gains access to environment-related information from 9,600 companies around the world, and the project promotes mutual exchange among companies and investors. As Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance has joined the CDP as a signatory, it has gained the right to request the disclosure of information for investment activities, and thus, it has access to the information related to the environmental management of companies, as a financial institution.

Declaration of endorsement for TCFD

In February 2021, Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance declared its endorsement for TCFD (Taskforce on Climate-related Financial Disclosures).
Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance identifies risks arising from its business activities that may affect the environment, including the provision of insurance products and services, complies with the TCFD recommendations to systematically manage those risks, and transparently discloses environmental information. In addition, the company will capitalize on various communication channels such as workshops, public hearings, seminars, and forums associated with eco-friendly activities to reinforce communication with stakeholders on environmental management.

Acquired an environmental management system (ISO 14001) certification

As an international standard for environmental management systems established by a technical committee of the International Organization for Standardization (ISO), this is a system that objectively evaluates and certifies environmental management systems throughout business activities.

  • Certifying body: Korea Productivity Center Quality Assurance
  • Certification period: June 10, 2019 - June 09, 2022 (3 years)
Selected as an outstanding company for weather management by the Korea Meteorological Administration

In 2018, Hyundai Marine & Fire Insurance was selected as an outstanding company for weather management by the Korea Meteorological Administration for the first time in the finance and insurance industry. The Korea Meteorological Administration's outstanding companies for weather management acknowledges the creation of added values and the acquisition of safety from weather and climate disaster by using weather information for business management.

  • Certifying body: Korea Meteorological Administration
  • Certification details: Certification as an outstanding company for weather management (first in the finance and insurance industry)
  • Certification period: October 26, 2021 - October 25, 2024 (3 years)
